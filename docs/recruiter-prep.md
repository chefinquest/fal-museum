# Recruiter Prep: fal Software Engineer, Growth

## One-sentence goal

I want the recruiter to leave believing: **Andrew already thinks and ships like a fal growth engineer — fast prototypes, technical taste, customer empathy, and a real bias toward distribution.**

## Role translation

fal's posting is not a generic SWE role. It is a hybrid of:

- prototype engineer
- developer advocate
- growth experimenter
- technical writer
- customer-facing debugger
- product-minded founder type

The job description's center of gravity is: **turn generative media infrastructure into adoption.**

## Your strongest positioning

Use this frame:

> "What attracted me to this role is that it combines the things I keep naturally doing: building fast, figuring out new AI tooling, making demos people can actually understand, and turning rough infrastructure into something that creates momentum with users."

## Demo stack to show

### 1. FAL Museum

Repository: `https://github.com/chefinquest/fal-museum`

What it proves:

- I can ship a working browser demo quickly.
- I can use multiple fal models, not just call one endpoint.
- I understand that demos need story, polish, docs, and provenance.
- I can talk about the follow-up growth loop: analytics, CTAs, distribution, blog/teardown, and iteration.

Talk track:

> "I wanted to build something that wasn't just 'here's an image generation API call.' The app is a small interactive museum where each wall is generated through a different fal model. I kept model and prompt metadata in the app because for developer tools, provenance matters — people want to understand how it was made and how they could reproduce it."

### 2. Hermes/Chefin setup

What it proves:

- I can set up an always-on AI development environment.
- I can wire together tools, credentials, CLIs, GitHub, Google, FAL, and local workflows.
- I think in systems and automation, not one-off hacks.

Talk track:

> "I also built my own always-on agent environment so I can move faster. It can run tools, generate media through fal, manage repos, and help me iterate. The point isn't that the agent does everything; it's that I like building leverage into my workflow."

### 3. fal/genmedia troubleshooting

What it proves:

- I can debug broken developer tooling.
- I don't stop at package failure; I trace upstream repos, releases, checksums, and working alternatives.
- This maps directly to customer-facing debugging in the role.

Talk track:

> "When I tried setting up genmedia, the npm package pointed to a dead GitHub repo. I traced it to the maintained fal-ai-community/genmedia-cli repo, installed the correct release binary, verified the checksum, and got generation working. That kind of thing is exactly what I expect to be doing with prospects and early users: unblock them fast and turn it into better docs or tooling."

## Your likely weaknesses — and how to neutralize them

### Weakness: not an obvious growth-engineering title on resume

Do not apologize. Reframe:

> "I haven't had the exact title 'growth engineer,' but the work pattern is familiar: build fast, ship a demo, measure the response, talk to users, and improve the path to activation. That's why this role stood out — it matches how I like to operate."

### Weakness: analytics stack may be less formal

Own it, then show speed:

> "I may not know your exact stack on day one, whether that's Looker, Amplitude, warehouse SQL, or something else. But I am comfortable getting to the metric by whatever path is fastest — SQL, CSVs, logs, or an API. For this role, I know the key is not dashboard beauty; it's acquisition cost, activation, and what experiment we run next."

### Weakness: portfolio/demo may still need public polish

Say the truth and turn it into a roadmap:

> "The demo is at prototype stage. If I had another day, I'd deploy it publicly, add event tracking for first interaction and docs clicks, add a fal CTA, and write the teardown thread. That's actually how I'd approach the role: ship the core experience, instrument, distribute, iterate."

## Questions recruiter may ask

### "Why fal?"

Answer:

> "fal feels like the infrastructure layer for the next wave of creative AI products. What I like is that the product is deeply technical, but the adoption motion depends on making developers and creative teams feel the magic quickly. That engineering-plus-distribution challenge is exactly what I want."

### "Why this role specifically?"

Answer:

> "I don't want to only sit behind a ticket queue. I want to build things that create visible movement: demos, docs, experiments, customer conversations, and product feedback loops. This role seems designed for someone who likes ambiguous, high-leverage work and can ship without a lot of hand-holding."

### "What have you built with fal?"

Answer:

> "I built an interactive 3D museum where each artwork comes from a different fal model family. I also set up and debugged local fal tooling, including the fal CLI and genmedia workflow. The important part is not just the app — it's that I can go from unfamiliar tooling to working demo and explainable process quickly."

### "Are you comfortable being customer-facing?"

Answer:

> "Yes. I like translating technical systems into something people can use. In this role, I would expect customer conversations to be part debugging, part product discovery, and part trust-building. I enjoy that mix."

### "Can you relocate / be in San Francisco?"

Prepare your real answer before the call. The posting is San Francisco and mentions relocation assistance. Be direct and enthusiastic if true.

## Questions you should ask them

Ask 2-3 of these:

1. "What does success look like in the first 30, 60, and 90 days for this growth engineer role?"
2. "Which acquisition or activation bottleneck is most important for fal right now?"
3. "How does the team decide whether a demo, blog post, or experiment worked?"
4. "What kinds of prospects or developers would I be working with most often?"
5. "How much of the role is writing/demo creation versus customer debugging versus analytics?"
6. "What are examples of growth experiments that have worked well at fal?"

## Call structure

### First 2 minutes

- Be warm and direct.
- Say you're excited because this role matches your natural operating style.
- Mention you built a fal-focused demo specifically to explore the role.

### Middle of call

- Keep answers concrete.
- Name the artifacts: FAL Museum, Hermes/Chefin workflow, genmedia debugging.
- Always connect artifacts back to role requirements.

### End of call

Say:

> "I'm very interested. If useful, I can send the repo/demo and a short write-up. I'm especially excited about the chance to build things that help developers experience fal quickly and then measure what actually drives activation."
